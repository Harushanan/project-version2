Our Journey

1983

Foundation Established

Tamil Information Centre was founded in response to the growing need for accurate information about the Tamil community in Sri Lanka.

1985

First Human Rights Report

Published our first comprehensive human rights report documenting violations in Sri Lanka.

1987

UN Recognition

Gained consultative status with the United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC).

1990

Geneva Office Opened

Established a permanent office in Geneva to enhance international advocacy efforts.

1994

Archival Collection Started

Began systematic collection and preservation of historical documents and materials.

1998

Digital Documentation Project

Initiated digitization of historical records for preservation and accessibility.

2001

Peace Process Documentation

Comprehensive documentation of the Sri Lankan peace process and negotiations.

2004

Tsunami Relief Archives

Documented relief efforts and community responses to the Indian Ocean tsunami.

2009

War Documentation Project

Launched extensive documentation of the final phase of the civil war.

2010

Memory Project Initiated

Started collecting personal testimonies and oral histories from survivors.

2013

30th Anniversary

Celebrated 30 years of service with special exhibitions and publications.

2015

UNHRC Advocacy

Intensive advocacy during the UN Human Rights Council sessions in Geneva.

2018

Digital Archive Launch

Launched our online digital archive accessible to researchers worldwide.

2020

Pandemic Documentation

Documented the impact of COVID-19 on Tamil communities globally.

2021

Virtual Exhibitions

Launched virtual exhibitions to reach global audiences during travel restrictions.

2023

40th Anniversary

Celebrated 40 years with major publications and international conferences.

2025

New Headquarters

Opened new state-of-the-art archival and research facility.
